After The USSR

On Christmas Day, 1991, the Soviet flag was lowered for the last time. The next morning, 25.3 million Russians woke as a part of a large diaspora. Where did they go?

Built with by Yaniv Goldobin
Published: December 12, 2018

When Michael Gorbachev stepped down as president of the USSR it was one of the most diverse countries in the world, home to over 253 million people belonging to over 100 ethnicities.

Emigration policies during the Soviet Era were stricly limited, averaging only 3,000 per year in the mid-1980’s. Trying to travel without explicit permission from the government was punishable by prison and death. Your odds of getting a pass to go were ridiculously low.

When the curtain finally came down, it gave rise to 15 new nations, some experiencing sovereignty for the first time in history.

The Russian population outside of Russia (the 25.3 million) had to decide whether to move back to the soviet homeland, or restart elsewhere entirely. Let's see what they did...
