After The USSR

On Christmas Day, 1991, the Soviet flag was lowered for the last time. The next morning, 25.3 million Russians woke as a part of a large diaspora. Where did they go?

On Christmas Day, December 25th, 1991, Mikhail Gorbachev resigned from his position as President of the Soviet Union. That evening, at 7:32 pm, the Soviet flag was lowered from the Kremlin for the last time. The next morning, 25.3 million Russians became a part of a large diaspora without moving an inch or leaving their homes.

14 new independent nations emerged. Some experiencing sovereignty for the first time.
